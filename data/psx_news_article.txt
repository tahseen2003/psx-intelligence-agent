HEADLINE: Banking Sector Faces Regulatory Storm as SBP Announces
          Surprise Capital Adequacy Review

ISLAMABAD — In a move that caught market participants off guard,
the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) announced late today that it will
conduct a comprehensive review of capital adequacy requirements
for all scheduled banks effective June 1, 2026.

Sources close to the central bank revealed that the new framework
may require banks to increase their Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)
from the current 11.5% to 14.5% — a significant jump that could
force major banks including HBL, UBL, and MCB to raise additional
capital or restrict dividend payouts.

"This is a major headwind for the banking sector," said Khalid
Mehmood, Chief Analyst at Arif Habib Securities. "Banks that
reported strong Q1 profits may see those gains wiped out by the
need to set aside additional capital buffers."

⚠️ KEY CONCERNS FOR BANKING SECTOR:

1. DIVIDEND RISK: HBL, which recently declared a strong Q1 profit
   of PKR 18.2 billion, may be forced to cut its final dividend
   by up to 40% to meet the new CAR requirements. Analysts had
   previously expected HBL to maintain its generous dividend policy.

2. PROFITABILITY PRESSURE: The expected 50 basis point rate CUT
   in the upcoming May 26 SBP meeting is now UNLIKELY, according
   to three senior economists. Instead, the SBP may HOLD rates
   steady or even RAISE them by 25 basis points to manage inflation
   which unexpectedly rose to 9.2% in April 2026.

3. FOREIGN INVESTOR SENTIMENT: Despite reports of continued foreign
   buying in banking stocks, multiple brokerage houses confirm that
   foreign institutional investors have actually been NET SELLERS
   in the banking sector for the past week, offloading approximately
   USD 8.5 million worth of banking shares. The reported "net inflows"
   primarily went into cement and fertilizer stocks, NOT banking.

4. NPL CONCERNS: Non-performing loans in the banking sector rose
   by 18% quarter-over-quarter, reaching PKR 1.2 trillion. This
   data, which was not reflected in the bullish Q1 earnings reports,
   suggests that asset quality is deteriorating rapidly.

ENERGY SECTOR — CONTRARIAN VIEW:

Contrary to the bearish outlook on energy stocks, industry sources
indicate that the government is finalizing a new petroleum pricing
policy that could increase domestic oil prices by 8-12%. This would
significantly benefit OGDC and PPL, whose stock prices have been
declining on global oil price concerns.

Furthermore, OGDC's Shaheen Block discovery in Balochistan is
expected to add 15,000 barrels per day to production starting
Q3 2026, which has not yet been priced into the stock.

CEMENT SECTOR WARNING:

While cement dispatches showed a 15% YoY increase, industry sources
warn that this growth is unsustainable. The government's austerity
measures under the IMF program are expected to slow down public
sector construction projects by 25-30% in H2 2026. LUCK and DGKC
may see demand compression starting August 2026.

MARKET OUTLOOK — BEARISH REVISION:

Multiple research houses have revised their KSE-100 target DOWNWARD
from 82,000-84,000 to 72,000-75,000 for June 2026, citing:
- Unexpected regulatory tightening
- Rising inflation concerns
- Potential interest rate increase
- Deteriorating banking sector asset quality

"Investors should exercise extreme caution," warned Fatima Rizvi,
Head of Research at JS Global. "The market euphoria around banking
earnings is misplaced. We recommend reducing banking sector exposure
immediately and shifting to defensive sectors like pharmaceuticals
and utilities."
