📈 MARKET OVERVIEW
-----------------------------------------
The KSE-100 index closed at 78,956 points, gaining 711 points (+0.91%)
in today's session. The market opened with bullish momentum driven by
strong quarterly earnings from the banking sector. Foreign investors
showed renewed interest with a net inflow of USD 12.3 million, the
highest single-day inflow in the past 3 months.

Total market capitalization reached PKR 9.45 trillion, up from
PKR 9.38 trillion in the previous session.

📊 SECTOR PERFORMANCE
-----------------------------------------
BANKING SECTOR:
The banking sector led the rally with a 2.1% gain. HBL surged 3.4%
to PKR 168.50 after reporting Q1 2026 profit of PKR 18.2 billion,
beating analyst estimates by 12%. UBL gained 2.8% to PKR 285.00 on
the back of strong net interest margin expansion. MCB and ABL also
posted gains of 1.9% and 1.5% respectively.

Analyst consensus: Banking sector outlook remains BULLISH for Q2 2026
due to high interest rate environment benefiting net interest income.

ENERGY SECTOR:
The energy sector showed mixed performance. OGDC declined 1.2% to
PKR 98.40 amid concerns over declining crude oil prices globally.
PPL fell 0.8% to PKR 82.30. However, HUBC gained 1.5% on strong
power generation numbers.

Oil prices dropped to USD 68.50/barrel (Brent Crude), putting
pressure on exploration and production companies.

CEMENT SECTOR:
Cement stocks showed strength with LUCK gaining 2.3% to PKR 892.00
on reports of increased construction activity in southern Pakistan.
DGKC rose 1.7% and MLCF gained 1.1%. Cement dispatches for April
2026 were up 15% year-over-year.

FERTILIZER SECTOR:
ENGRO gained 1.8% to PKR 345.00 after announcing a special dividend
of PKR 15 per share. FFC remained flat at PKR 112.50.

TECHNOLOGY SECTOR:
TRG Pakistan continued its volatile trend, falling 3.2% to PKR 78.50.
Systems Limited gained 0.9% to PKR 615.00 on expectations of
strong IT export numbers for Q1 2026.
